Academic Support

Advising

Advisors are available to help you discover and develop your interests as you progress toward a WSU degree. They can help you evaluate career options, discuss academic opportunities, map your course progression, and connect you to other resources.

  • Tutoring
    Meet with experienced peers who can help you be successful in specific courses and subjects.
  • Academic Mentoring
    Find opportunities to be a mentor or be mentored by WSU students, faculty, and staff.
  • Academic Workshops
    Attend sessions to get best tips and practices from experts who are invested in your success.
  • UNIV Courses
    Take a class to build skills necessary for academic success.
  • Student Success Programs
    Explore the specialized programs at WSU that are collectively dedicated to helping you—and all—students flourish throughout their college education.
  • Academic Coaching
    Develop skills and build confidence through individual coaching sessions focused on time management, learning strategies, goal-setting, motivation, and more.
  • Writing Program
    Get individualized support to develop your written communication skills and learn how to interpret and meet instructions and the goals of your assignments.

Opportunities

Undergraduate Research

Every student in every major can experience hands-on research, scholarship, and creative activity with a faculty mentor. Participating in "undergraduate research" will help you to create and reinforce knowledge in your field. Undergraduate researchers have opportunities to share the results of their efforts, seek awards, and network with professionals in your discipline. Undergraduate research experience is also a great addition to your resume.

  • Distinguished Scholarships
    Pursue prestigious, nationally competitive awards that fund your WSU education or studies abroad.
  • Common Reading
    Share a common text on important contemporary topics with your professors and classmates.
  • Internships
    Get relevant experience in an industry or service position with intentional learning goals.
  • Student Employment
    Make connections with campus and regional employers to build your skills and resume.
  • National Student Exchange
    Explore alternatives to study for up to one calendar year at a college other than WSU.
